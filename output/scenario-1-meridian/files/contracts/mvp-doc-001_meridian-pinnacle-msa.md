# MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

**Agreement No.:** MSA-2023-0147
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2023

---

## PARTIES

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the Effective Date by and between:

**MERIDIAN CORPORATION**, a Delaware corporation, with its principal place of business at 4500 Technology Drive, Suite 300, San Jose, California 95134 ("Meridian" or "Client"),

and

**PINNACLE INDUSTRIES, INC.**, a California corporation, with its principal place of business at 1200 Innovation Boulevard, Fremont, California 94538 ("Pinnacle" or "Service Provider").

Meridian and Pinnacle are each referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Meridian is a leading developer of advanced manufacturing technologies, including proprietary thermal compression molding processes protected by United States Patent No. 9,876,543 ("the '543 Patent") and related intellectual property;

WHEREAS, Pinnacle is a manufacturing and engineering services company with expertise in precision component fabrication, thermal processing, and industrial automation;

WHEREAS, Meridian desires to engage Pinnacle to provide certain engineering, manufacturing support, and technical consulting services in connection with Meridian's advanced materials processing operations;

WHEREAS, Pinnacle desires to provide such services to Meridian on the terms and conditions set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements herein contained, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

**1.1** "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other Party, whether orally, in writing, or by inspection, including but not limited to trade secrets, proprietary technology, business plans, financial information, customer lists, manufacturing processes, and technical data. Confidential Information shall include the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

**1.2** "Deliverables" means all work product, reports, analyses, designs, prototypes, documentation, and other materials created or developed by Pinnacle in the performance of the Services.

**1.3** "Intellectual Property" or "IP" means all patents, patent applications, trademarks, trade names, copyrights, trade secrets, know-how, inventions, designs, processes, techniques, algorithms, software, data, and any other proprietary rights.

**1.4** "Services" means the engineering, manufacturing support, technical consulting, and related services to be performed by Pinnacle under this Agreement, as further described in each Statement of Work.

**1.5** "Statement of Work" or "SOW" means a written document executed by both Parties that describes specific Services to be performed, Deliverables to be provided, timelines, fees, and other project-specific terms, substantially in the form attached hereto as Exhibit A.

**1.6** "Term" means the period beginning on the Effective Date and ending on December 31, 2025, unless earlier terminated in accordance with Article 10.

---

## ARTICLE 2: SCOPE OF SERVICES

**2.1 General Scope.** Pinnacle shall provide Services to Meridian as described in individual Statements of Work executed by both Parties from time to time during the Term. Each SOW shall be incorporated into and governed by this Agreement.

**2.2 Standard of Care.** Pinnacle shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards and practices applicable to similar services. Pinnacle shall assign qualified personnel with appropriate expertise and experience to perform the Services.

**2.3 Service Categories.** The Services may include, but are not limited to, the following categories:

(a) Manufacturing process engineering and optimization;
(b) Technical consulting on thermal compression molding and related processes;
(c) Prototype development and testing;
(d) Quality assurance and testing services;
(e) Equipment calibration and maintenance support;
(f) Process documentation and technical writing;
(g) Training of Meridian personnel on manufacturing techniques.

**2.4 Exclusions.** Pinnacle shall not, without the prior written consent of Meridian:

(a) Perform any Services that would require access to or use of Meridian's patented thermal compression molding technology (US Patent 9,876,543) beyond the scope expressly authorized in a SOW;
(b) Subcontract any portion of the Services to third parties;
(c) Modify or reverse-engineer any Meridian equipment, processes, or technology.

**2.5 Change Orders.** Either Party may request changes to the scope of Services under an existing SOW. Any such changes shall be documented in a written Change Order signed by authorized representatives of both Parties, specifying the nature of the change, any impact on schedule, and any adjustment to fees.

---

## ARTICLE 3: COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT

**3.1 Annual Contract Value.** The total annual compensation payable to Pinnacle for Services rendered under this Agreement shall not exceed Two Million Four Hundred Thousand Dollars ($2,400,000.00) per calendar year during the Term, unless otherwise agreed in writing by the Parties.

**3.2 Fee Structure.** Fees for Services shall be established in each SOW and may include:

(a) **Time and Materials:** Hourly rates for personnel as set forth in Exhibit B (Rate Card), currently ranging from $175/hour for junior engineers to $425/hour for senior technical specialists;
(b) **Fixed Price:** A fixed fee for specified Deliverables, as agreed in the applicable SOW;
(c) **Milestone-Based:** Payments tied to achievement of specified milestones, as agreed in the applicable SOW.

**3.3 Invoicing.** Pinnacle shall submit detailed invoices to Meridian on a monthly basis, within fifteen (15) calendar days following the end of each calendar month. Each invoice shall include:

(a) A description of Services performed during the invoicing period;
(b) The names and roles of personnel who performed Services;
(c) Hours worked by each individual (for time and materials engagements);
(d) Itemized expenses eligible for reimbursement;
(e) Reference to the applicable SOW.

**3.4 Payment Terms.** Meridian shall pay all undisputed invoices within thirty (30) calendar days of receipt of a proper invoice. Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less.

**3.5 Expenses.** Meridian shall reimburse Pinnacle for reasonable, pre-approved, out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of Services, subject to Meridian's expense reimbursement policy. Expenses exceeding Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00) per item require prior written approval from Meridian.

**3.6 Taxes.** All fees are exclusive of applicable sales, use, value-added, or similar taxes. Meridian shall be responsible for all such taxes, excluding taxes based on Pinnacle's income.

## ARTICLE 4: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**4.1 Meridian Pre-Existing IP.** Meridian retains all right, title, and interest in and to all Intellectual Property owned or controlled by Meridian prior to the Effective Date or developed independently of this Agreement, including without limitation US Patent 9,876,543 and all related patents, patent applications, and trade secrets (collectively, "Meridian IP"). Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as granting Pinnacle any license or rights to Meridian IP, except as expressly set forth in a SOW.

**4.2 Pinnacle Pre-Existing IP.** Pinnacle retains all right, title, and interest in and to all Intellectual Property owned or controlled by Pinnacle prior to the Effective Date or developed independently of this Agreement ("Pinnacle IP").

**4.3 Work Product Ownership.** All Deliverables and work product created by Pinnacle in the performance of Services under this Agreement shall be the sole and exclusive property of Meridian. Pinnacle hereby irrevocably assigns to Meridian all right, title, and interest in and to such Deliverables, including all Intellectual Property rights therein.

**4.4 Work Made for Hire.** To the extent any Deliverable constitutes a "work made for hire" under the United States Copyright Act, it shall be deemed a work made for hire owned by Meridian. To the extent any Deliverable does not qualify as a work made for hire, Pinnacle hereby assigns to Meridian all copyright and other intellectual property rights in such Deliverable.

**4.5 Cooperation.** Pinnacle shall cooperate with Meridian, at Meridian's expense, to execute all documents and take all actions reasonably necessary to perfect, protect, and enforce Meridian's rights in the Deliverables and work product.

**4.6 Restrictions on Use.** Pinnacle shall not use any Meridian IP, Deliverables, or Confidential Information for any purpose other than the performance of Services under this Agreement. Pinnacle shall not develop, manufacture, or sell any product or service that incorporates or is based upon Meridian IP without the prior written consent of Meridian.

## ARTICLE 5: CONFIDENTIALITY

**5.1 Obligations.** Each Party agrees to: (a) hold the other Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party; and (c) use such Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing its obligations or exercising its rights under this Agreement.

**5.2 Exceptions.** The obligations under Section 5.1 shall not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving Party; (b) was known to the receiving Party prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed by the receiving Party without use of the disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or (d) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure.

**5.3 Required Disclosures.** A receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the receiving Party: (a) gives the disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement (to the extent legally permitted); and (b) cooperates with the disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

**5.4 Duration.** The confidentiality obligations under this Article 5 shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years; provided, however, that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

**7.1 Pinnacle Indemnification.** Pinnacle shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Meridian and its directors, officers, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) any breach by Pinnacle of its representations, warranties, or obligations under this Agreement; (b) any negligent or willful act or omission of Pinnacle or its personnel in the performance of Services; (c) any claim that the Services or Deliverables infringe a third party's Intellectual Property rights; or (d) any violation of applicable law by Pinnacle.

**7.3 Indemnification Procedure.** The indemnified Party shall: (a) promptly notify the indemnifying Party in writing of any claim; (b) grant the indemnifying Party sole control of the defense and settlement of such claim; and (c) cooperate with the indemnifying Party in the defense of such claim, at the indemnifying Party's expense. The indemnified Party may participate in the defense at its own expense.

**9.1 Required Coverage.** During the Term and for a period of two (2) years thereafter, Pinnacle shall maintain the following insurance coverage:

(a) Commercial General Liability: $2,000,000 per occurrence / $5,000,000 aggregate;
(b) Professional Liability (Errors & Omissions): $5,000,000 per claim / $5,000,000 aggregate;
(c) Workers' Compensation: As required by applicable law;
(d) Commercial Automobile Liability: $1,000,000 combined single limit.

**9.2 Certificates.** Pinnacle shall provide Meridian with certificates of insurance evidencing the required coverage upon request.

## ARTICLE 10: TERM AND TERMINATION

**10.1 Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until December 31, 2025, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Article 10 (the "Initial Term"). The Agreement may be renewed for successive one (1) year periods upon written agreement of both Parties.

**10.2 Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice to the other Party.

**10.3 Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach; (b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed for a substantial part of its assets; or (c) ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

**10.4 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement:

(a) Pinnacle shall immediately cease performing Services and delivering Deliverables;
(b) Pinnacle shall return or destroy all Meridian Confidential Information within thirty (30) days;
(c) Meridian shall pay Pinnacle for all Services satisfactorily performed and expenses properly incurred prior to the effective date of termination;
(d) All provisions that by their nature should survive termination shall survive, including Articles 4, 5, 7, 8, and 11.

## ARTICLE 11: GENERAL PROVISIONS

**11.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California, without regard to its conflict of laws provisions.

**11.2 Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved as follows:

(a) **Negotiation:** The Parties shall first attempt to resolve the dispute through good-faith negotiation between senior executives within thirty (30) days of written notice of the dispute;
(b) **Mediation:** If negotiation fails, the Parties shall submit the dispute to non-binding mediation administered by JAMS in San Jose, California;
(c) **Litigation:** If mediation fails, either Party may pursue litigation in the federal or state courts located in Santa Clara County, California. Each Party consents to the exclusive jurisdiction of such courts.

**11.3 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with all Exhibits, SOWs, and Change Orders, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, representations, and understandings.

**11.4 Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

**11.5 Waiver.** The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such provision or the right to enforce it at a later time.

**11.6 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**11.7 Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that either Party may assign this Agreement in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of its assets.

**11.8 Notices.** All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by: (a) personal delivery; (b) nationally recognized overnight courier; (c) registered or certified mail, return receipt requested; or (d) email with confirmation of receipt, to the addresses set forth above or such other address as a Party may designate in writing.

**11.9 Independent Contractor.** Pinnacle is an independent contractor, and nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employer-employee, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the Parties.

**11.10 Force Majeure.** Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) to the extent such failure or delay results from causes beyond the reasonable control of such Party, including acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, epidemics, government actions, or failures of third-party telecommunications or power supply.

**11.11 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
