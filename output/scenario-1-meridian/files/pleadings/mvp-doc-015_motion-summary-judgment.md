## MEMORANDUM OF POINTS AND AUTHORITIES

### I. INTRODUCTION

Meridian respectfully requests that this Court grant partial summary judgment on the issue of literal infringement of Claims 1, 3, and 7 of the '543 Patent. The undisputed evidence establishes that Pinnacle's AutoForge System satisfies every element of these claims. Pinnacle's own engineering documents, produced in discovery, confirm that the AutoForge System was designed using Meridian's patented technology and incorporates each claimed element of the thermal compression molding system and method.

The key facts are not in dispute:

- Pinnacle had full access to Meridian's patented technology through the Technology License Agreement and Technical Package.
- Pinnacle's AutoForge System uses a multi-stage compression assembly, thermal compression boundary layer management, independent stage control, and dynamic boundary layer adjustment — precisely the elements claimed in the '543 Patent.
- Pinnacle's own internal technical specifications describe the AutoForge System using terminology and process descriptions that map directly onto the claim language of the '543 Patent.
- Pinnacle's technical expert, when deposed, conceded that the AutoForge System includes each structural element recited in Claim 1.

### III. LEGAL STANDARD

Summary judgment is appropriate "if the movant shows that there is no genuine dispute as to any material fact and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law." Fed. R. Civ. P. 56(a). In patent infringement cases, literal infringement requires that the accused product or process contain each and every element of the asserted claims. *Vitronics Corp. v. Conceptronic, Inc.*, 90 F.3d 1576, 1581 (Fed. Cir. 1996).

The determination of literal infringement is a two-step process: (1) the claims must be properly construed to determine their scope and meaning; and (2) the properly construed claims must be compared to the accused product or process. *Markman v. Westview Instruments, Inc.*, 52 F.3d 967, 976 (Fed. Cir. 1995) (en banc), aff'd, 517 U.S. 370 (1996).

Claim construction is a matter of law for the Court. *Markman*, 517 U.S. at 391. Whether the accused product meets each claim limitation is a factual determination. *Bai v. L & L Wings, Inc.*, 160 F.3d 1350, 1353 (Fed. Cir. 1998). Summary judgment on infringement is appropriate where no reasonable jury could find non-infringement. *KCJ Corp. v. Kinetic Concepts, Inc.*, 223 F.3d 1351, 1358 (Fed. Cir. 2000).

### IV. ARGUMENT

#### A. The Court Has Already Construed the Disputed Claim Terms

On January 28, 2026, this Court issued its Claim Construction Order (Dkt. No. 89), construing the disputed terms of the '543 Patent. The Court construed "thermal compression boundary layer" to mean "the thermally managed zone at the interface between the compression tooling and the composite workpiece, where controlled heat transfer and pressure distribution occur during compression." The Court construed "multi-stage compression molding" to mean "a compression process comprising two or more discrete compression stages applied sequentially to the workpiece, each stage having independently controllable parameters."

These constructions are now the law of the case and control the infringement analysis.

#### B. Pinnacle's AutoForge System Literally Infringes Claim 1

Claim 1 recites four elements. The undisputed evidence shows the AutoForge System satisfies each:

**Element 1: "A multi-stage compression assembly configured to apply sequential compression forces to a composite material workpiece."**

Pinnacle's own technical specification describes the AutoForge System as employing "sequential multi-stage compression with independently controlled press actuators." (Ex. E, Section 3.2.) Dr. Brennan confirmed at deposition that the AutoForge System applies "compression in multiple sequential stages." (Brennan Dep. 87:14-22.) Dr. Patel's expert analysis confirms this element is met. (Patel Decl. Paragraphs 18-24.)

**Element 2: "A thermal management subsystem configured to establish and maintain a thermal compression boundary layer at the interface between the compression assembly and the workpiece during each compression stage."**

The AutoForge technical specification describes a "Thermal Zone Management Module" that creates and maintains "controlled thermal zones at the tooling-workpiece interface." (Ex. E, Section 3.4.) This corresponds precisely to the claimed "thermal compression boundary layer" as construed by the Court. Dr. Patel confirms the AutoForge thermal management subsystem creates and maintains a thermal compression boundary layer as claimed. (Patel Decl. Paragraphs 25-31.)

**Element 3: "A control system configured to independently regulate the temperature profile and compression force during each stage of the multi-stage compression process."**

The AutoForge specification describes an "Integrated Process Control Unit" that provides "independent parametric control of temperature ramp rates, dwell temperatures, compression force magnitude, and compression velocity for each compression stage." (Ex. E, Section 3.6.) This meets the claimed element. (Patel Decl. Paragraphs 32-38.)

**Element 4: "Wherein the thermal compression boundary layer is dynamically adjusted during transitions between compression stages to optimize material flow and consolidation."**

The AutoForge specification describes "dynamic thermal zone transitions" between compression stages, specifically noting that "boundary zone parameters are continuously adjusted during stage transitions to ensure optimal resin flow and fiber consolidation." (Ex. E, Section 3.8.) Dr. Patel confirms this satisfies the claim limitation. (Patel Decl. Paragraphs 39-45.)

Pinnacle has not identified any element of Claim 1 that is absent from the AutoForge System. No reasonable jury could find that the AutoForge System does not literally infringe Claim 1.

#### C. Pinnacle's AutoForge System Literally Infringes Claim 3

Claim 3 depends from Claim 1 and adds the limitation of "a sensor array configured to monitor thermal compression boundary layer characteristics in real time and provide feedback to the control system for dynamic adjustment."

The AutoForge System includes an "Integrated Sensor Network" comprising "thermocouple arrays, pressure transducers, and capacitive proximity sensors distributed across the tooling-workpiece interface" that provide "real-time feedback to the process control unit for closed-loop dynamic adjustment of thermal zone parameters." (Ex. E, Section 4.2.) Dr. Patel confirms this satisfies the additional limitation of Claim 3. (Patel Decl. Paragraphs 46-52.)

#### D. Pinnacle's AutoForge System Literally Infringes Claim 7

Claim 7 is a method claim reciting three steps. The undisputed evidence shows the AutoForge System practices each step:

**Step 1: "Establishing a thermal compression boundary layer at a first temperature and a first compression force."** The AutoForge operational procedure begins with "Stage 1 Initialization: Establish thermal zone at target temperature T1 and apply compression force F1." (Ex. E, Section 5.1.) (Patel Decl. Paragraphs 53-57.)

**Step 2: "Transitioning to a second compression stage by adjusting the thermal compression boundary layer to a second temperature and a second compression force."** The AutoForge procedure continues with "Stage Transition: Adjust thermal zone parameters to Stage 2 targets (T2, F2) with controlled ramp rates." (Ex. E, Section 5.3.) (Patel Decl. Paragraphs 58-61.)

**Step 3: "Controlling the rate of transition between stages to maintain continuous material flow."** The AutoForge specification expressly addresses "Transition Rate Control" and states that "transition rates are managed to ensure continuous and uniform material flow throughout the compression cycle." (Ex. E, Section 5.4.) (Patel Decl. Paragraphs 62-65.)

### V. CONCLUSION

The undisputed evidence — including Pinnacle's own engineering documents and deposition testimony — establishes that the AutoForge System literally satisfies each and every element of Claims 1, 3, and 7 of the '543 Patent. There is no genuine dispute of material fact, and Meridian is entitled to partial summary judgment on literal infringement.

Meridian respectfully requests that the Court grant this Motion and enter an order finding that Pinnacle's AutoForge Multi-Stage Compression Platform literally infringes Claims 1, 3, and 7 of US Patent No. 9,876,543.
