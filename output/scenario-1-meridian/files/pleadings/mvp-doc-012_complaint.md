## COMPLAINT FOR PATENT INFRINGEMENT

---

Plaintiff Meridian Corporation ("Meridian" or "Plaintiff"), by and through its attorneys of record, Baker & Associates LLP, hereby files this Complaint for Patent Infringement against Defendant Pinnacle Industries, Inc. ("Pinnacle" or "Defendant"), and alleges as follows:

---

## NATURE OF THE ACTION

1. This is an action for patent infringement under 35 U.S.C. Sections 271 et seq. arising from Pinnacle's unauthorized manufacture, use, sale, offer for sale, and importation of products and processes that infringe United States Patent No. 9,876,543 ("the '543 Patent"), entitled "Multi-Stage Thermal Compression Molding System and Method for Advanced Composite Materials."

2. Meridian seeks injunctive relief, compensatory damages, enhanced damages for willful infringement, and all other relief to which it is entitled.

---

## THE PARTIES

3. Plaintiff Meridian Corporation is a corporation duly organized and existing under the laws of the State of Delaware, with its principal place of business at 4500 Technology Drive, Suite 300, San Jose, California 95134. Meridian is a leading developer and manufacturer of advanced manufacturing technologies, specializing in thermal compression molding systems for composite materials.

4. Defendant Pinnacle Industries, Inc. is a corporation duly organized and existing under the laws of the State of California, with its principal place of business at 1200 Innovation Boulevard, Fremont, California 94538. Upon information and belief, Pinnacle is a manufacturing and engineering services company that manufactures and sells precision components using, among other technologies, thermal compression molding processes.

## JURISDICTION AND VENUE

## THE PATENT-IN-SUIT

8. On June 14, 2022, the United States Patent and Trademark Office duly and lawfully issued US Patent No. 9,876,543, entitled "Multi-Stage Thermal Compression Molding System and Method for Advanced Composite Materials," to inventor Dr. Jonathan R. Whitfield. The '543 Patent was assigned to Meridian Corporation. A true and correct copy of the '543 Patent is attached hereto as Exhibit A and incorporated herein by reference.

9. The '543 Patent contains twenty (20) claims, including fourteen (14) independent and dependent claims (Claims 1-14) directed to a multi-stage thermal compression molding system and method for processing advanced composite materials. The patented technology addresses critical limitations in prior art molding processes by providing a novel approach to managing thermal compression boundary layers and achieving precise multi-stage compression profiles.

10. The '543 Patent relates to groundbreaking innovations in thermal compression molding technology developed by Meridian over a period of more than five (5) years, at a research and development cost exceeding Twelve Million Dollars ($12,000,000.00).

11. The technology claimed in the '543 Patent enables the production of advanced composite components with superior structural integrity, dimensional accuracy, and surface finish compared to conventional molding processes. The patented multi-stage compression approach achieves these results by precisely controlling the thermal compression boundary layer during sequential compression stages, resulting in optimized material flow and consolidation.

12. Meridian is the sole owner by assignment of all right, title, and interest in the '543 Patent, including the right to sue for infringement and recover damages therefor.

## FACTUAL BACKGROUND

### The Parties' Business Relationship

13. In or about November 2022, the Parties entered into a Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("NDA") to facilitate discussions regarding a potential business relationship involving Meridian's thermal compression molding technology and Pinnacle's manufacturing capabilities.

14. In January 2023, the Parties entered into a Master Services Agreement ("MSA") under which Pinnacle agreed to provide engineering, manufacturing support, and technical consulting services to Meridian in connection with Meridian's advanced materials processing operations.

15. In March 2023, the Parties entered into a Technology License Agreement ("TLA") under which Meridian granted Pinnacle a limited, non-exclusive license to use the Licensed Technology (as defined therein) solely within the Field of Use — the manufacture of precision-molded composite components for use exclusively in the automotive heat exchanger market segment, limited to components with a maximum dimension not exceeding 450 millimeters in any axis.

16. The TLA expressly prohibited Pinnacle from: (a) using the Licensed Technology outside the Field of Use; (b) sublicensing the technology to third parties; (c) modifying, adapting, or creating derivative works of the Licensed Technology without Meridian's written consent; and (d) reverse engineering the Licensed Technology beyond the Technical Package expressly provided by Meridian.

### Pinnacle's Development of the AutoForge System

17. Upon information and belief, beginning in or about September 2023, Pinnacle began developing a manufacturing system it calls the "AutoForge Multi-Stage Compression Platform" (the "AutoForge System"), which incorporates and utilizes the thermal compression molding technology claimed in the '543 Patent.

18. Upon information and belief, Pinnacle developed the AutoForge System using knowledge, know-how, and trade secrets obtained from Meridian under the NDA, MSA, and TLA, including technical information from the Technical Package delivered under the TLA.

19. Upon information and belief, the AutoForge System employs a multi-stage thermal compression molding process that practices one or more claims of the '543 Patent, including but not limited to the use of: (a) a thermal compression boundary layer management system; (b) sequential multi-stage compression profiles; (c) real-time thermal feedback control during compression stages; and (d) composite material flow optimization through controlled boundary layer manipulation.

20. Upon information and belief, Pinnacle has marketed, sold, and deployed the AutoForge System to customers in the aerospace, defense, and industrial manufacturing sectors — applications far outside the limited Field of Use authorized under the TLA.

21. Upon information and belief, Pinnacle began marketing the AutoForge System to third-party customers no later than March 2024, and has generated substantial revenue from the sale and licensing of AutoForge Systems and related services.

### Discovery of Infringement

22. In or about April 2025, Meridian became aware that Pinnacle was marketing the AutoForge System at the Advanced Manufacturing Expo in Chicago, Illinois. Meridian's engineers who attended the trade show observed demonstrations of the AutoForge System that appeared to utilize Meridian's patented thermal compression molding technology.

23. In May 2025, Meridian retained technical experts to analyze publicly available information about the AutoForge System, including marketing materials, technical data sheets, and customer testimonials. The analysis confirmed that the AutoForge System practices the methods and systems claimed in the '543 Patent.

24. On June 2, 2025, Meridian sent Pinnacle a written notice of infringement, identifying the '543 Patent and requesting that Pinnacle cease and desist from all infringing activities. Pinnacle responded on June 20, 2025, denying infringement and asserting that the AutoForge System employs "independently developed technology."

25. On July 15, 2025, Meridian terminated the Technology License Agreement pursuant to Section 7.2 thereof, based on Pinnacle's material breach of the Field of Use restrictions and other license limitations.

## COUNT I: INFRINGEMENT OF US PATENT NO. 9,876,543

26. Meridian incorporates by reference the allegations of paragraphs 1 through 25 above.

27. Pinnacle has infringed and continues to infringe the '543 Patent by making, using, selling, offering to sell, and/or importing the AutoForge System and products manufactured using the AutoForge System, in violation of 35 U.S.C. Section 271(a).

28. On information and belief, the AutoForge System literally infringes at least Claims 1 through 14 of the '543 Patent. In the alternative, the AutoForge System infringes at least Claims 1 through 14 under the doctrine of equivalents, as the AutoForge System performs substantially the same function, in substantially the same way, to achieve substantially the same result as each element of the asserted claims.

29. **Claim 1** of the '543 Patent recites, inter alia:

> "A thermal compression molding system for processing composite materials, comprising:
> a multi-stage compression assembly configured to apply sequential compression forces to a composite material workpiece;
> a thermal management subsystem configured to establish and maintain a thermal compression boundary layer at the interface between the compression assembly and the workpiece during each compression stage;
> a control system configured to independently regulate the temperature profile and compression force during each stage of the multi-stage compression process;
> wherein the thermal compression boundary layer is dynamically adjusted during transitions between compression stages to optimize material flow and consolidation."

30. On information and belief, the AutoForge System includes each and every element recited in Claim 1, including a multi-stage compression assembly, a thermal management subsystem that creates thermal compression boundary layers, a control system for independent stage regulation, and dynamic boundary layer adjustment during stage transitions.

31. **Claim 3** of the '543 Patent recites, inter alia, the system of Claim 1 further comprising "a sensor array configured to monitor thermal compression boundary layer characteristics in real time and provide feedback to the control system for dynamic adjustment." On information and belief, the AutoForge System includes real-time sensor monitoring and feedback control of boundary layer characteristics.

32. **Claim 7** of the '543 Patent recites, inter alia, "a method for multi-stage thermal compression molding of composite materials, comprising the steps of: establishing a thermal compression boundary layer at a first temperature and a first compression force; transitioning to a second compression stage by adjusting the thermal compression boundary layer to a second temperature and a second compression force; and controlling the rate of transition between stages to maintain continuous material flow." On information and belief, the AutoForge System practices each step of Claim 7.

33. Pinnacle's infringement has been and continues to be willful. Pinnacle had actual knowledge of the '543 Patent by virtue of: (a) the Technology License Agreement, which expressly referenced the '543 Patent; (b) Meridian's June 2, 2025 cease and desist letter; and (c) Pinnacle's access to the Technical Package provided under the TLA. Despite this knowledge, Pinnacle continued to manufacture, market, and sell the infringing AutoForge System.

34. Meridian has been and continues to be damaged by Pinnacle's infringement in an amount to be determined at trial, but in no event less than a reasonable royalty.
