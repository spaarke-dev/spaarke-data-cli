## III. DAMAGES FRAMEWORK

### A. Lost Profits Analysis

**Applicable Period:** March 2024 (first AutoForge sale) through present and continuing.

Based on information available to date, Pinnacle has sold or licensed approximately 14 AutoForge Systems at an average price of approximately $1.8 million per unit, generating estimated gross revenue of approximately $25.2 million. Additionally, Pinnacle has generated estimated service and maintenance revenue of approximately $4.5 million related to AutoForge installations.

Under a lost profits theory, Meridian must demonstrate: (1) demand for the patented product; (2) absence of acceptable non-infringing substitutes; (3) manufacturing and marketing capability to exploit the demand; and (4) the amount of profit Meridian would have made. *Panduit Corp. v. Stahlin Bros. Fibre Works*, 575 F.2d 1152 (6th Cir. 1978).

**Strengths:** Meridian can demonstrate demand (the '543 Patent technology is commercially valuable) and manufacturing capability.

**Weaknesses:** Some AutoForge sales were in aerospace/defense markets where Meridian does not currently compete, making it harder to prove Meridian would have captured those sales. Additionally, there are competing (non-infringing) thermal processing technologies that could qualify as acceptable substitutes.

**Lost Profits Estimate:** $12-18 million (covers only the sales Meridian can prove it would have made).

### B. Reasonable Royalty Analysis

Under Georgia-Pacific, we would conduct a hypothetical negotiation analysis to determine the royalty rate the parties would have agreed upon at the time infringement began. Key factors include:

1. **Established Royalty:** The TLA provided a 4% royalty on Net Sales within the Field of Use. This is a strong starting point, but we can argue the rate should be higher for unauthorized, out-of-field use.

2. **Relationship of the Parties:** They were licensor/licensee. The existing license rate is a floor, not a ceiling, for unauthorized use.

3. **Commercial Success:** The AutoForge System has been commercially successful, with rapid market adoption and strong customer demand.

4. **Portion of Profit Attributable to Patent:** The thermal compression boundary layer technology is a core differentiator of the AutoForge System, not a minor feature.

**Reasonable Royalty Rate Estimate:** 8-12% of AutoForge Net Sales (double to triple the licensed rate, reflecting unauthorized use, willfulness premium, and absence of field-of-use restrictions).

**Reasonable Royalty Damages Estimate:** $2.4-3.6 million on current sales, plus ongoing royalties on future sales.

### C. Enhanced Damages

Under 35 U.S.C. Section 284, the Court may enhance damages up to three times for willful infringement. Given the strength of our willfulness evidence (actual knowledge via TLA, post-cease-and-desist continuation), we believe a 2x enhancement is supportable.

### D. Injunctive Relief Value

A permanent injunction would require Pinnacle to cease manufacturing and selling the AutoForge System, effectively removing a competitor from the market. The threat of injunction has significant settlement value independent of damages, as Pinnacle would need to redesign its product or exit the market entirely.

Under *eBay Inc. v. MercExchange*, 547 U.S. 388 (2006), the four-factor test applies. We assess our injunction likelihood as medium-high, based on: (1) irreparable harm (ongoing infringement, lost market position); (2) inadequacy of monetary damages (difficult to quantify long-term competitive harm); (3) balance of hardships (favors Meridian as the innovator); (4) public interest (neutral).

## IV. SCENARIO ANALYSIS

### A. Best Case Scenario — $45 Million

**Assumptions:**
- Favorable claim construction on both disputed terms
- Court finds literal infringement of Claims 1, 3, and 7 on summary judgment
- Willful infringement finding at trial
- Lost profits awarded for domestic sales + reasonable royalty for markets where lost profits cannot be proven
- 2x enhancement for willfulness
- Permanent injunction granted
- Ongoing royalty of 10% on future sales

**Calculation:**
- Lost profits: $18M
- Reasonable royalty (non-lost-profit sales): $3.5M
- Enhancement (1.5x on total): $10.75M
- Injunctive relief value (capitalized future royalty stream): $12.75M
- **Total: ~$45M**

**Probability Assessment:** 15-20%. This scenario requires everything to go our way — favorable claim construction, summary judgment on infringement, willfulness finding, and full damages at trial.

### B. Expected Case Scenario — $28 Million

**Assumptions:**
- Favorable claim construction (our constructions adopted)
- Infringement found at trial (not on summary judgment)
- Willful infringement found, but enhancement limited to 1.5x
- Reasonable royalty damages at 10% of Net Sales
- No injunction, but ongoing royalty imposed
- Pinnacle's invalidity defense fails

**Calculation:**
- Reasonable royalty (past damages): $3.0M
- Enhancement (1.5x): $1.5M
- Lost profits (partial): $10M
- Ongoing royalty (capitalized, 5-year horizon at $5M/year sales): $13.5M
- **Total: ~$28M**

**Probability Assessment:** 50-60%. This is the most likely outcome if the case proceeds to trial and we obtain favorable claim construction.

### C. Worst Case Scenario — $8 Million

**Assumptions:**
- Mixed claim construction (one term construed favorably, one narrowly)
- Infringement found only under doctrine of equivalents (not literal)
- No willfulness finding
- Reasonable royalty only (no lost profits)
- Moderate royalty rate (6% of Net Sales)
- No injunction or ongoing royalty
- Pinnacle's invalidity defense partially succeeds (some claims invalidated, but Claims 1 and 7 survive)

**Calculation:**
- Reasonable royalty (past): $1.8M
- Reasonable royalty (ongoing 3-year projection): $6.2M
- **Total: ~$8M**

**Probability Assessment:** 20-25%. This scenario reflects a case where claim construction goes partially against us and damages are limited.

### D. Tail Risk — Complete Loss

There is a small but non-trivial risk (5-10%) that all asserted claims are invalidated or claim construction is sufficiently adverse that no infringement is found. In this scenario, Meridian recovers nothing and may be liable for Pinnacle's attorneys' fees if the case is found exceptional. We assess this risk as low but not negligible.

## VI. SETTLEMENT NEGOTIATION STRATEGY

### Recommended Opening Position

- **Demand:** $50 million (gives room for negotiation)
- **Target:** $25-30 million
- **Minimum:** $18 million plus injunctive terms

### Non-Monetary Terms to Seek

In addition to monetary damages, we should seek:

1. **Permanent injunction** or consent decree restricting AutoForge sales outside the original Field of Use
2. **Ongoing royalty** of 10% on future AutoForge sales within any field
3. **Technology audit rights** to verify compliance
4. **Acknowledgment** of patent validity (prevents future challenges)
5. **Cross-license provisions** if Pinnacle has any relevant IP

### Timing

The optimal time to initiate settlement discussions is **immediately after a favorable Markman ruling** (expected Q1 2026). A favorable claim construction ruling will significantly increase our leverage and Pinnacle's motivation to settle.

If settlement discussions are initiated before Markman, we should set a minimum floor of $18 million, recognizing the uncertainty.

---

## VII. RECOMMENDATION

We recommend targeting a settlement in the **$25-30 million range**, to be negotiated primarily after the Markman ruling. The weighted expected value of the case at trial is approximately $26.25 million, and a settlement in this range provides certainty while avoiding trial risk and additional litigation costs ($2-3M in projected costs through trial).

If Pinnacle approaches us with a settlement offer before Markman, we should not accept anything below $18 million without strong justification (e.g., significant invalidity evidence we have not yet identified).
