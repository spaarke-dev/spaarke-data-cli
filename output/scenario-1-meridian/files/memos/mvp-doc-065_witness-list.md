## I. PURPOSE

This memorandum presents our preliminary witness list for trial, currently scheduled for October 19, 2026. The list includes eight (8) fact witnesses and two (2) expert witnesses. ## III. EXPERT WITNESSES

### 9. Dr. Robert Patel — Manufacturing Process Expert

**Affiliation:** Stanford University, Professor of Mechanical Engineering and Materials Science
**Engagement:** Technical infringement expert
**Expected Testimony:**
- Technical background on thermal compression molding technology
- Element-by-element comparison of AutoForge System against Claims 1-14 of '543 Patent
- Opinion that AutoForge literally infringes all asserted claims
- Comparison of AutoForge architecture to Meridian's Technical Package
- Technical rebuttal of Pinnacle's non-infringement arguments

**Estimated Time:** 3-4 hours (direct), 2-3 hours (cross)
**Preparation Notes:** Dr. Patel is an experienced expert witness with excellent credentials. His preliminary analysis strongly supports literal infringement. His expert report is due May 30, 2026. Key preparation task: ensure his testimony is accessible to a lay jury — use demonstratives, animations, and simplified diagrams. Need to prepare for Daubert challenge (unlikely to succeed given his qualifications, but should be ready).

**Compensation:** $650/hour (anticipated total: $80,000-$120,000)

**Affiliation:** California Institute of Technology, Professor of Materials Science and Engineering

Key preparation task: prepare her to explain why the prior art combinations proposed by Pinnacle fail — particularly the "no motivation to combine" and "no reasonable expectation of success" arguments.

**Compensation:** $700/hour (anticipated total: $90,000-$130,000)

## V. WITNESS ORDER RECOMMENDATION

**Plaintiff's Case-in-Chief (recommended order):**

1. James A. Whitfield (CEO) — set the stage, business context
2. Sarah Chen (GC) — licensing relationship, contract terms
3. Dr. Karen Liu (Licensing) — Technical Package delivery, Field of Use
4. Dr. Jonathan Whitfield (Inventor) — patent technology, development
5. Robert Maxwell (Manufacturing) — commercial implementation, trade show observation
6. Dr. Brennan (adverse) — admissions on AutoForge design
7. Lisa Monroe (adverse) — Technical Package distribution
8. Dr. Robert Patel (expert) — infringement analysis
9. Dr. Emily Nakamura (expert) — validity defense rebuttal
10. Damages expert (TBD) — damages calculation

**Rationale:** This order builds the narrative from business context (why the relationship existed) through contract terms (what was licensed and restricted) to technical evidence (how the patent was infringed) and finally damages. The adverse witnesses are placed between the fact and expert witnesses to create a powerful infringement sequence: Meridian's witnesses establish the technology and license, Pinnacle's own witnesses confirm access and design choices, and the experts provide the technical and legal conclusions.
