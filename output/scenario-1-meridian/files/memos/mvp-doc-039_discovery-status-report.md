### B. Deficiency Analysis

Pinnacle's productions to date are **materially deficient** in several respects:

1. **AutoForge Engineering Documents:** Pinnacle has produced only limited technical specifications for the AutoForge System. Notably absent are: (a) detailed design documents for the thermal management subsystem; (b) source code for the process control software; (c) testing and validation records; (d) engineering change orders. These documents are directly responsive to our Document Request Nos. 4, 7, 12, and 15.

2. **Financial Records:** Pinnacle has not produced any financial records related to AutoForge revenue, costs, or profitability, despite our Document Request No. 18 specifically requesting these documents. Pinnacle's counsel has indicated that financial records will be produced "in a subsequent production," but no date has been provided.

3. **Customer Communications:** Pinnacle has produced only redacted marketing materials. It has not produced actual customer communications, sales records, purchase orders, or licensing agreements for AutoForge Systems, which are responsive to Document Request Nos. 20-23.

4. **ESI Deficiencies:** Pinnacle has not produced any ESI from the following custodians identified in our initial disclosures request: (a) Dr. Thomas Brennan (Chief Engineer, AutoForge lead); (b) Jennifer Walsh (VP of Engineering); (c) David Kim (VP of Business Development). These are key custodians whose documents are essential to our infringement and willfulness claims.

### C. Motion to Compel Status

On January 5, 2026, we filed a motion to compel production of the deficient categories identified above (Dkt. No. 78). Pinnacle filed its opposition on January 12, 2026 (Dkt. No. 82), arguing that: (a) certain AutoForge documents are protected by trade secret; (b) financial records are premature before liability is established; (c) customer communications contain third-party confidential information. We filed our reply on January 16, 2026 (Dkt. No. 85). The hearing is scheduled for January 28, 2026, before Magistrate Judge Patricia Hernandez.

**Assessment:** We expect the motion to be **granted in substantial part**. The protective order already in place addresses Pinnacle's trade secret concerns, and courts routinely order production of financial records in patent cases. We anticipate the Court will order production within 30 days of the ruling.

## IV. PRIVILEGE LOG STATUS

### A. Meridian Privilege Log

We have identified 1,843 documents as responsive but privileged. The privilege log is approximately 65% complete and will be served by February 15, 2026.

Key categories of privileged documents:
- Attorney-client communications regarding '543 Patent prosecution (312 documents)
- Attorney-client communications regarding TLA negotiation (187 documents)
- Attorney work product — litigation preparation (456 documents)
- Attorney-client communications regarding infringement investigation (388 documents)
- Mixed privilege/responsive — under review (500 documents)

### B. Pinnacle Privilege Log

Pinnacle served a privilege log with its second production identifying 892 withheld documents. We have reviewed this log and identified the following concerns:

1. **Overbroad privilege claims:** 143 entries appear to claim privilege over documents that include non-attorney recipients without explanation of the common interest doctrine or agency relationship.

2. **Insufficient descriptions:** 78 entries contain descriptions too vague to assess the privilege claim (e.g., "Email re: business matters").

3. **Crime-fraud exception:** 12 entries appear to involve communications about developing the AutoForge System while the TLA was in effect, potentially implicating the crime-fraud exception to the extent they reflect advice on how to use licensed technology outside the licensed field.

We plan to challenge these privilege assertions after resolving the pending motion to compel.
