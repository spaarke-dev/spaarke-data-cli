Having conducted the Initial Case Management Conference on October 6, 2025, and having considered the Joint Case Management Statement filed by the parties (Dkt. No. 38), the Court hereby enters the following Case Management and Scheduling Order:

---

## I. NATURE OF THE CASE

This is a patent infringement action in which Plaintiff Meridian Corporation alleges that Defendant Pinnacle Industries, Inc. infringes Claims 1-14 of U.S. Patent No. 9,876,543, entitled "Multi-Stage Thermal Compression Molding System and Method for Advanced Composite Materials," through its AutoForge Multi-Stage Compression Platform. Defendant denies infringement and asserts invalidity of the patent-in-suit.

---

## II. JURISDICTION AND JURY DEMAND

The Court has subject matter jurisdiction under 28 U.S.C. Sections 1331 and 1338(a). Both parties have consented to the jurisdiction of this Court. Plaintiff has demanded a jury trial.

## IV. DISCOVERY PROVISIONS

### A. Electronically Stored Information (ESI)

1. The parties shall meet and confer within fourteen (14) days of this Order to establish an ESI protocol governing the format, scope, and procedures for the production of electronically stored information.

2. ESI shall be produced in the format in which it is ordinarily maintained, or in a reasonably usable format, consistent with the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure and the Northern District of California Patent Local Rules.

3. The parties shall use reasonable search terms and custodian limitations, to be agreed upon or submitted to the Court for resolution.

### B. Protective Order

4. The parties shall submit a stipulated protective order within twenty-one (21) days of this Order. If the parties cannot agree on terms, each party shall submit a proposed protective order, and the Court will resolve any disputes.

### C. Discovery Disputes

5. Before filing any discovery motion, the parties must meet and confer in good faith, in person or by telephone, to attempt to resolve the dispute. The moving party must certify compliance with this requirement in any discovery motion.

6. Discovery disputes shall be submitted to Magistrate Judge Patricia Hernandez pursuant to 28 U.S.C. Section 636(b)(1)(A).

7. Discovery letters (not to exceed five pages) may be submitted in lieu of formal motions for discovery disputes that can be resolved on an expedited basis.

## VI. SETTLEMENT

The parties are encouraged to explore settlement at the earliest practicable time. The Court is available for settlement conferences upon request of any party. The parties may also pursue private mediation at any time.

The parties shall participate in at least one settlement conference or mediation session before the dispositive motion deadline.
