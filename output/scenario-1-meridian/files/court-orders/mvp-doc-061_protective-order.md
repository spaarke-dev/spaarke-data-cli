## STIPULATED PROTECTIVE ORDER

---

Plaintiff Meridian Corporation ("Meridian") and Defendant Pinnacle Industries, Inc. ("Pinnacle") (collectively, the "Parties"), by and through their respective attorneys of record, hereby stipulate and agree, and the Court hereby orders, as follows:

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 This Stipulated Protective Order ("Protective Order") is entered to prevent the unauthorized disclosure and use of Confidential Information produced or disclosed during the course of this litigation.

1.2 This Protective Order applies to all documents, testimony, information, and other materials produced, disclosed, or otherwise made available by any Party or non-party in connection with this action, whether produced voluntarily or pursuant to formal discovery (collectively, "Discovery Material").

1.3 Nothing in this Protective Order shall prevent any Party from using or disclosing its own Confidential Information without restriction.

---

## 2. CONFIDENTIALITY DESIGNATIONS

Discovery Material may be designated at one of three tiers of confidentiality:

### 2.1 Tier 1: "CONFIDENTIAL"

Information that the Designating Party reasonably and in good faith believes constitutes confidential business information, the disclosure of which could cause competitive harm. This includes, but is not limited to: non-public financial data, business strategies, customer lists, vendor relationships, and personnel information.

### 2.2 Tier 2: "HIGHLY CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEYS' EYES ONLY" (AEO)

Information that the Designating Party reasonably and in good faith believes constitutes highly sensitive confidential business information, trade secrets, or proprietary technical information, the disclosure of which could cause significant competitive harm. This includes, but is not limited to: detailed product specifications, manufacturing processes, proprietary algorithms, pricing models, customer contracts, and detailed financial records.

### 2.3 Tier 3: "HIGHLY CONFIDENTIAL — SOURCE CODE"

Source code, object code, firmware, and related technical documentation that the Designating Party reasonably and in good faith believes constitutes trade secret source code. This designation is reserved for actual software source code and closely related materials.

---

## 3. ACCESS TO DESIGNATED MATERIAL

### 3.1 Tier 1 — "CONFIDENTIAL" Material

May be disclosed to:

(a) Outside counsel of record and their staff (attorneys, paralegals, legal assistants, secretaries);
(b) In-house counsel of the Receiving Party who are actively involved in the management of this litigation;
(c) Retained expert witnesses and consultants, subject to execution of the Acknowledgment in Exhibit A;
(d) Court reporters and videographers during depositions;
(e) The Court and Court personnel;
(f) Witnesses during depositions and trial, to the extent necessary for examination;
(g) E-discovery vendors and document review contractors, subject to confidentiality agreements.

### 3.2 Tier 2 — "ATTORNEYS' EYES ONLY" Material

May be disclosed to:

(a) Outside counsel of record and their staff;
(b) Retained expert witnesses and consultants, subject to execution of the Acknowledgment in Exhibit A;
(c) Court reporters and videographers during depositions;
(d) The Court and Court personnel;
(e) E-discovery vendors and document review contractors, subject to confidentiality agreements.

**NOT** to be disclosed to: In-house counsel, employees, officers, or directors of the Receiving Party, except as expressly ordered by the Court.

### 3.3 Tier 3 — "SOURCE CODE" Material

May be disclosed to:

(a) A maximum of two (2) outside attorneys of record actively working on the case;
(b) One (1) retained expert witness, subject to execution of the Source Code Acknowledgment in Exhibit B;
(c) The Court and Court personnel.

**Source Code Review Procedures:**
(i) Source code shall be reviewed only at the offices of the Designating Party's outside counsel, or at a mutually agreed secure location;
(ii) Source code shall be made available on a standalone computer with no internet connection, no USB ports, and no ability to copy, transmit, or photograph the source code;
(iii) The reviewing expert may take handwritten notes referencing specific portions of the source code, but may not copy code verbatim in excess of one hundred (100) lines in any single note;
(iv) Review sessions shall not exceed eight (8) hours per day;
(v) All notes shall be treated as "HIGHLY CONFIDENTIAL — SOURCE CODE" material.

---

## 4. DESIGNATION PROCEDURES

### 4.1 Marking Documents

(a) Documents shall be designated by placing the appropriate legend on each page:
   - "CONFIDENTIAL"
   - "HIGHLY CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEYS' EYES ONLY"
   - "HIGHLY CONFIDENTIAL — SOURCE CODE"

(b) For electronically produced documents, the designation may appear as a Bates prefix, a metadata field, or a separate designation log.

### 4.2 Marking Deposition Testimony

(a) During a deposition, a Party may designate testimony as confidential by stating the designation on the record.

(b) Within fourteen (14) days after receipt of the deposition transcript, any Party may designate additional portions of the transcript by written notice to all parties.

### 4.3 Good Faith Designation

All designations must be made in good faith. A Party shall not designate material at a higher tier than is reasonably warranted.

---

## 5. CHALLENGING DESIGNATIONS

5.1 A Receiving Party may challenge any confidentiality designation by providing written notice to the Designating Party, identifying the specific material and the basis for the challenge.

5.2 Within fourteen (14) days of receiving the challenge notice, the Designating Party shall either: (a) agree to re-designate the material; or (b) provide a written response explaining the basis for the designation.

5.3 If the parties cannot resolve the dispute, the challenging Party may file a motion with the Court. The Designating Party bears the burden of establishing that the designation is proper.

5.4 Pending resolution of any challenge, the material shall be treated at the designation level assigned by the Designating Party.

---

## 6. USE OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

6.1 All Confidential Information produced or disclosed under this Protective Order shall be used solely for the prosecution, defense, and resolution of this action, and for no other purpose.

6.2 No Party shall use Confidential Information obtained in this action for any business purpose, competitive advantage, or in any other litigation or proceeding, without the prior written consent of the Designating Party or an order of the Court.

---

## 7. COURT FILINGS

7.1 If a Party seeks to file any document containing Confidential Information with the Court, the filing Party shall:

(a) File the document under seal or with the confidential portions redacted from the public filing;
(b) Comply with Civil Local Rule 79-5 and any applicable standing orders regarding sealed filings;
(c) Provide notice to the Designating Party at least three (3) business days before filing, to allow the Designating Party to seek additional protective measures if desired.

7.2 The Court recognizes the strong presumption of public access to judicial records and will consider any sealing request under the applicable legal standard.

---

## 8. INADVERTENT PRODUCTION

8.1 The inadvertent production of any document shall not constitute a waiver of any applicable privilege, protection, or confidentiality designation, consistent with Federal Rule of Evidence 502(b) and (d).

8.2 If a Producing Party discovers that it has inadvertently produced privileged or work product material, it shall promptly notify the Receiving Party in writing, identify the material, and state the basis for the privilege or protection. Upon receipt of such notice, the Receiving Party shall promptly return, sequester, or destroy all copies of the identified material and shall not use or disclose such material pending resolution of the privilege claim.

---

## 9. TERMINATION AND SURVIVAL

9.1 Within sixty (60) days after the final resolution of this action (including all appeals), each Receiving Party shall, at the Designating Party's election: (a) return all Confidential Information to the Designating Party; or (b) destroy all Confidential Information and certify such destruction in writing.

9.2 Notwithstanding Section 9.1, outside counsel may retain one (1) archival copy of all pleadings, correspondence, and work product, subject to the continuing obligations of this Protective Order.

9.3 The obligations of this Protective Order shall survive the final resolution of this action.

---

## 10. MODIFICATION

This Protective Order may be modified by written stipulation of the Parties approved by the Court, or by order of the Court upon motion of any Party.

---

## 11. THIRD-PARTY DISCOVERY

If any non-party is required to produce documents or testimony in this action that the non-party considers confidential, the non-party may designate such material under this Protective Order upon written notice to the Parties. The non-party shall be bound by the terms of this Protective Order with respect to any Confidential Information it receives.
