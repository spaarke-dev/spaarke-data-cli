## ORDER ON PLAINTIFF'S MOTION TO COMPEL PRODUCTION OF ELECTRONICALLY STORED INFORMATION

---

Before the Court is Plaintiff Meridian Corporation's ("Meridian") Motion to Compel Production of Electronically Stored Information (Dkt. No. 78), filed January 5, 2026. Defendant Pinnacle Industries, Inc. ("Pinnacle") filed its Opposition on January 12, 2026 (Dkt. No. 82). Meridian filed its Reply on January 16, 2026 (Dkt. No. 85). The Court held a hearing on the motion on January 28, 2026, at which counsel for both parties appeared and were heard.

Having considered the parties' papers, the arguments of counsel, and the record in this case, the Court rules as follows:

---

## I. BACKGROUND

Meridian seeks to compel Pinnacle to produce four categories of documents that Meridian contends are responsive to its discovery requests but have not been produced:

1. **AutoForge engineering and design documents** (responsive to Document Request Nos. 4, 7, 12, and 15), including detailed design specifications for the thermal management subsystem, process control software source code, testing and validation records, and engineering change orders;

2. **Financial records** related to AutoForge revenue, costs, and profitability (responsive to Document Request No. 18);

3. **Customer communications, sales records, and licensing agreements** for AutoForge Systems (responsive to Document Request Nos. 20-23);

4. **ESI from key custodians** Dr. Thomas Brennan, Jennifer Walsh, and David Kim (responsive to multiple document requests).

Pinnacle opposes the motion, arguing that: (a) certain AutoForge technical documents are protected trade secrets; (b) financial records should not be required before a liability determination; (c) customer communications contain third-party confidential information; and (d) the disputed custodians' files were searched and found to contain no responsive documents.

---

## II. LEGAL STANDARD

Under Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 26(b)(1), parties may obtain discovery regarding "any nonprivileged matter that is relevant to any party's claim or defense and proportional to the needs of the case." The scope of discovery is broad, and doubts regarding relevance are generally resolved in favor of allowing discovery. *Shoen v. Shoen*, 5 F.3d 1289, 1292 (9th Cir. 1993).

The party resisting discovery bears the burden of showing that the discovery is not relevant or is unduly burdensome. *Blankenship v. Hearst Corp.*, 519 F.2d 418, 429 (9th Cir. 1975).

---

## III. ANALYSIS

### A. AutoForge Engineering and Design Documents

Pinnacle's primary objection is that its AutoForge engineering documents constitute trade secrets that should not be disclosed. The Court disagrees. The AutoForge System is the accused instrumentality in this patent infringement action. Documents describing its design, architecture, and engineering are directly relevant to the central issue of infringement and must be produced.

The Stipulated Protective Order entered on October 20, 2025 (Dkt. No. 55) provides three tiers of confidentiality protection: "Confidential," "Highly Confidential — Attorneys' Eyes Only," and "Highly Confidential — Source Code." This framework is specifically designed to protect trade secrets and other sensitive business information during patent litigation while allowing the parties to pursue their claims and defenses.

Pinnacle may designate its AutoForge engineering documents at the appropriate confidentiality tier under the Protective Order. This adequately addresses Pinnacle's trade secret concerns.

**Regarding source code:** Meridian has requested production of the source code for the AutoForge process control system software. Source code discovery is routinely permitted in patent infringement cases where the software is relevant to the infringement analysis. *See Oracle Am., Inc. v. Google LLC*, No. C 10-03561 WHA (N.D. Cal.). The Court ORDERS production of the source code, subject to the "Highly Confidential — Source Code" provisions of the Protective Order. Source code review shall take place at Pinnacle's counsel's office, on a standalone workstation provided by Pinnacle, in sessions of no more than eight (8) hours per day, with no more than two (2) reviewers present at any time (one attorney, one technical expert).

**The motion is GRANTED as to Category 1.**

### B. Financial Records

Pinnacle argues that financial records should not be produced before a finding of liability. This argument has been consistently rejected by courts in this district. Financial records relating to sales, revenue, costs, and profits from the accused product are relevant to damages and are discoverable during fact discovery, not limited to post-liability proceedings. *See Lucent Techs., Inc. v. Gateway, Inc.*, 580 F.3d 1301, 1324 (Fed. Cir. 2009).

Pinnacle shall produce financial records related to AutoForge Systems, including but not limited to: revenue by customer, cost of goods sold, gross and net profit margins, research and development costs, and licensing revenue. These documents may be designated under the appropriate tier of the Protective Order.

**The motion is GRANTED as to Category 2.**

### C. Customer Communications

Pinnacle argues that customer communications contain third-party confidential information and that production would violate Pinnacle's confidentiality obligations to its customers. The Court acknowledges this concern but finds that the Protective Order's "Highly Confidential — Attorneys' Eyes Only" designation provides adequate protection. To the extent Pinnacle has contractual obligations to notify customers before disclosing their information, Pinnacle shall provide such notifications within ten (10) days of this Order.

Customer communications, including sales proposals, purchase orders, contract terms, and post-sale correspondence relating to AutoForge Systems, are relevant to both infringement (commercial use of the accused product) and damages (revenue and pricing).

**The motion is GRANTED as to Category 3**, subject to Pinnacle's right to notify affected customers and designate documents at the "Highly Confidential — Attorneys' Eyes Only" tier.

### D. ESI from Key Custodians

Pinnacle's assertion that Dr. Brennan, Ms. Walsh, and Mr. Kim possess no responsive documents is not credible. Dr. Brennan is the chief engineer who led the AutoForge development team. Ms. Walsh, as VP of Engineering, oversaw the project. Mr. Kim, as VP of Business Development, managed AutoForge sales. The notion that none of these individuals have any email, documents, or other ESI related to the AutoForge System is implausible.

The Court ORDERS Pinnacle to conduct a new, comprehensive search of the email accounts, personal drives, OneDrive accounts, and shared drives accessible to Dr. Brennan, Ms. Walsh, and Mr. Kim, using search terms to be agreed upon by the parties within ten (10) days of this Order. If the parties cannot agree on search terms, each party shall submit proposed terms to the Court, and the Court will resolve the dispute.

**The motion is GRANTED as to Category 4.**

---

## IV. SANCTIONS

Meridian has not requested sanctions, and the Court does not impose sanctions at this time. However, the Court cautions Pinnacle that continued obstruction of legitimate discovery may result in sanctions under Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 37, including monetary sanctions, adverse inference instructions, or preclusion of evidence.

---

## V. PRODUCTION DEADLINE

Pinnacle shall produce all documents responsive to Categories 1-4 within thirty (30) days of this Order (i.e., by February 27, 2026). Source code access shall be made available within forty-five (45) days of this Order.

---

## VI. CONCLUSION

For the foregoing reasons, Plaintiff's Motion to Compel (Dkt. No. 78) is GRANTED IN PART. Pinnacle shall produce the ordered documents in accordance with the deadlines and procedures set forth above.
